Kundli Matching for Marriage UK — Complete Guide to Kundali Milan in 2026

Kundli MatchingMarriageKundali MilanVedic AstrologyUK Families
Published May 16, 2026 · By Astrologer Rachit · 8 min read

Kundli matching — known in Sanskrit as Kundali Milan or Guna Milan — is the Vedic process of comparing two birth charts before marriage to assess cosmic compatibility. For millions of British-Indian, British-Pakistani and British-Sri Lankan families across the UK, it remains an essential step before finalising a marriage alliance. This guide covers everything you genuinely need to know — including what the scores actually mean and the critical factors that most automated online calculators miss entirely.

What Is Kundli Matching and Why Does It Matter?

A Kundli (birth chart) is a precise mathematical map of the positions of the planets, moon, sun and celestial houses at the exact moment of birth. Kundli matching involves comparing the charts of two people considering marriage across multiple compatibility categories — looking not just at whether they will get along, but at the long-term cosmic harmony of their union: will there be financial stability, emotional compatibility, physical health, children, and most critically — longevity of the marriage itself?

The Vedic sages who developed this system did so from thousands of years of observation of human relationships and their corresponding planetary patterns. It is not superstition — it is one of the most sophisticated compatibility assessment systems ever developed.

The Ashta Kuta System — Understanding the 36 Points

The most widely used Kundli matching system in the UK is the Ashta Kuta (eight qualities) system, which scores compatibility across eight distinct categories for a maximum of 36 points. Here is what each category assesses:

  • Varna (1 point) — Spiritual compatibility and ego levels. Checks whether both partners are spiritually aligned.
  • Vashya (2 points) — The ability to influence and attract each other. Governs mutual dominance and submission in the relationship.
  • Tara (3 points) — Health and wellbeing compatibility. Examines whether the marriage will be healthy for both partners.
  • Yoni (4 points) — Physical and sexual compatibility. Governs biological harmony between the couple.
  • Graha Maitri (5 points) — Mental compatibility and intellectual harmony. Governs friendship, respect and emotional understanding.
  • Gana (6 points) — Temperament compatibility. Classifies each person as Deva (divine), Manav (human) or Rakshasa (demon) temperament and checks harmony.
  • Bhakoot (7 points) — Emotional and financial compatibility. One of the most important categories — governs overall relationship harmony, prosperity and family growth.
  • Nadi (8 points) — Health and genetic compatibility. The highest-scoring category and the most critical health indicator in Vedic matching.

A score above 18 out of 36 is generally considered compatible for marriage. A score of 24 or above is considered highly compatible. A score below 18 traditionally indicates significant incompatibility and requires careful consideration and remedies.

What the Score Doesn't Tell You — The Critical Factors Most Calculators Miss

This is the most important section of this guide. The 36-point score is a starting framework — not a final verdict. Many automated online Kundli matching tools calculate the score and stop there. A genuine expert analysis goes much further:

Mangal Dosha: Whether either or both partners have Mangal Dosha must be assessed separately from the Guna Milan score. Two Mangliks marrying each other cancels the dosha — but the automated calculation needs to verify the specific placement, not just flag its presence.

Nadi Dosha Exception: The Nadi Dosha (Nadi score = 0) is the most feared in Kundli matching — traditionally indicating health problems and potential early death. However, Nadi Dosha is cancelled in multiple specific situations: if both partners have the same Rashi (moon sign) but different Nakshatras, or if they share the same Nakshatra but different Padas. Most automated tools flag Nadi Dosha without checking these cancellations.

Bhakoot Dosha: A Bhakoot Dosha (Bhakoot score = 0) is also cancellable in specific planetary conditions. Again, expert verification is essential before treating it as a dealbreaker.

7th House Analysis: Beyond the Guna Milan score, an expert astrologer examines the 7th house (house of marriage) in both charts in detail — its lord, any planets aspecting it, and the overall strength of the marriage indicator in both Kundlis.

Dasha Timing: Whether both partners are in planetary periods (Dashas) that support marriage is a separate but crucial consideration — a couple may be highly compatible but the timing may not be cosmically auspicious for the next two years.

Kundli Matching for Love Marriages in the UK

Kundli matching is often associated exclusively with arranged marriages, but Astrologer Rachit sees an increasing number of couples in London, Birmingham, Leicester and across the UK who have chosen each other through love and simply want the peace of mind of a Vedic compatibility assessment before committing to marriage.

For love marriages, the matching process is the same — but the conversation is different. Rather than deciding whether to proceed with the match, the focus shifts to identifying any cosmic challenges the couple may face and prescribing specific remedies to mitigate them. This gives the couple both reassurance and practical tools for navigating their relationship's specific planetary dynamics.

What to Bring to a Kundli Matching Session

For the most accurate Kundli matching, bring the following for both the prospective bride and groom: full date of birth; exact time of birth (from the birth certificate if possible); and city and country of birth. The time of birth is the most important factor — even a difference of a few minutes can change the ascendant (Lagna) and significantly affect the reading.
